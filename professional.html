Actual scientific activities

Since 2004, I spend my research time in the "Plant Fiber" from the UMR UGSF 8476 (CNRS and University of Lille). The main research topic of the team is using 'Omics' tools to investigate plant cell physiology and metabolism, with a particular focus on plant cell wall.

Plant cell wall degradation

I am particularly involved in the study of plant cell wall degradation. The approach we developped is based on metagenomics using bacterial or fungal molecular markers and high throughput sequencing.i) the composition and the evolution of the microflora population, ii) the kinetics of microbial colonization of plant material and iii) the composition and the evolution of the plant cell wall-degrading enzymes targeted to the different CAZy families.

Master international Plant Sciences

Since 2016, I am in charge of the direction of the Master international Plant Sciences of the university of lille. This Master‘s degree is an international MSc in Plant Sciences in which all courses, lab courses, Master’s dissertation and oral defence will be performed in english. It will be hold both in France and in Germany (Bonn). An ERASMUS Summer semester (March-October) is obligatory achieved in Bonn. Mobility is also possible during the M2-Summer semester for the research training period in laboratory.

 

Inovative pedagogy

Attentive in providing all possible help for the success of our students, we have developed digital tools to help learning. Among these tools, a set of tests allowing a self-evaluation have been created. These tests contain photographs to annotate and quizzes to test his knowledge in plant biology. These exercises are available online "cmveg.univ-lille1". A version adapted to the Moodle platform have been developped to use this resource as a part of a conventional teaching. The same concept was used to develop a self-assessment tool in cell biology.

The KANApe project proposes to develop a numerical assistance allowing student autonomy in the use of complex analytical devices commonly used in biology (HPLC, GC, capillary electrophoresis, multimode reader, thermocycler, ...). The solution is developped around homemade kiosks based on RASPBERRY Pi 3B motherboards equipped with a touchscreen in wich assistant tutorials based on a simple iconography enriched by textual explanations will be implemented. This project under development should see the first kiosks delivered for the start of September.
